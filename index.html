The report highlights the significant challenges faced by visually impaired students in accessing IT education, which is essential for their personal and professional development. Globally, there are 43 million blind people and 295 million with moderate to severe visual impairments. In Sri Lanka alone, 350,000 people are blind and an additional 340,000 have severe vision impairments. Current e-learning platforms often fall short in accommodating visually impaired students, as they predominantly rely on visual materials and interfaces. This reliance creates a competitive disadvantage for these students. Voice-based chatbots have emerged as a promising solution, using speech recognition and synthesis technology to provide an engaging and accessible educational experience. These chatbots can guide learners through content, answer questions, and assess knowledge through voice interactions. Despite their potential, many chatbots are not designed with visually impaired users in mind. Traditional chatbots often use less sophisticated datasets like AIML, while more versatile options like JSON are better for creating inclusive chatbots. While voice assistants like Alexa, Siri, and Cortana have successfully assisted visually impaired users in daily tasks, their integration into educational settings remains limited, especially in IT education. The lack of tailored support and mentoring in IT education for visually impaired students hinders their professional growth. Voice-based chatbots could bridge this gap by offering personalized learning recommendations and knowledge assessments. Emotion detection technology, such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs), can further enhance education by understanding students' emotional states, leading to a more supportive learning environment. The study underscores the importance of inclusive education and the role of technology in providing equitable learning opportunities for all students.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Voice-based Chatbot

The proposed research aims to address a gap in current chatbot systems. While some cater to educational content and accessibility for the visually impaired, they lack personalized learning paths. Voice assistants assist the visually impaired but lack educational features. Educational institution chatbots lack personalization and accessibility for the visually impaired. Our system aims to combine these strengths, offering tailored learning paths for all users, including the visually impaired.

Voice- answer detection and lesson series with basic programming

The first study explores voice-guided Tutor for visually impaired children, enhancing web app accessibility. Yet, there's a gap in teaching IT concepts. The second study uses physical flowcharts to teach programming,and also teaching algorithms and pseudo codes using audio based laerning system. And Created voice based answer selection platform for visually impared people. This is used to improve computational thinking knowledge.

Interactive Hand Gestures

There's a lack of research on teaching specialized IT skills to visually impaired learners. Current literature tends to overlook areas like BMIT, IAS, and programming, focusing more on general topics. There's minimal study on creating tools for visually impaired students, particularly those using gesture recognition technology. Closing this gap is vital for fair IT education. Researchers need to develop specialized programs and utilize gesture technology for interactive learning, advancing inclusive education for visually impaired learners.

Emotion Detection

The proposed system integrates various techniques to cater to the needs of visually impaired students, filling a gap in existing research. Unlike conventional approaches, it focuses on IT education and emotional engagement, addressing the unique requirements of this demographic. With features like phone accessibility enhancements and online emotion monitoring, it aims to improve accessibility and learning outcomes. This system represents a crucial advancement for visually impaired students, especially in Sri Lanka where such platforms are lacking.

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

In what ways can online learning platforms facilitate connections between visually impaired students and potential employers in the IT industry, promoting inclusivity and equal opportunities for employment?

This study addresses the significant educational and professional challenges faced by visually impaired students in the field of information technology (IT). These students struggle to access digital content, impacting their ability to learn and perform in IT courses, which undermines their confidence and reduces their job market opportunities. Current educational methods, relying on screen readers and tactile techniques, often fail to provide a comprehensive and engaging learning experience, particularly for complex IT subjects. The project's goal is to develop and evaluate a voice-based chatbot integrated into e-learning platforms to create a more tailored and accessible learning environment for visually impaired students. This chatbot will enable students to interact with content via voice commands, making the learning process more independent and personalized. It will offer module recommendations based on the user's knowledge level and interests, enhancing their educational experience. The research also focuses on teaching programming to visually impaired students who lack prior IT knowledge. To build their computational thinking skills, the study will initially teach flowcharts, algorithms, and pseudocode. Progress will be monitored through emotion and confidence detection, providing insights into students' emotional and confidence levels. This data will help educators adjust the curriculum to better meet students' needs. Additionally, the study explores novel pedagogical approaches, such as using tangible flowcharts and algorithms and incorporating gamification and activities to make learning more engaging. These methods aim to enhance students' understanding of computational thinking. The research will also examine ways to connect visually impaired students with IT industry employers. This involves creating a system where students can maintain profiles showcasing their skills, progress, and achievements, facilitating employer outreach for training and employment opportunities. The project aims to provide equitable opportunities for visually impaired students to succeed in the IT industry, bridging the gap between education and employment.


Proposed Solution

The main aim of the research is to provide an accessible online learning environment that uses voice-activated technologies to improve the quality of education for visually impaired students studying information technology (IT). This platform's goal is to remove the current obstacles to accessing digital content by offering easily accessible, interesting, and useful learning modules in important IT domains like security,business management, programming, and algorithms. The platform will facilitate a more inclusive and participatory learning environment by allowing visually impaired students to navigate and interact with the instructional content through voice commands by integrating voice assistants. Furthermore, the study intends to apply novel techniques for monitoring and evaluating visually impaired students' development using non-traditional metrics including mood and confidence detection. These discoveries will make it possible to modify instructional materials to better meet the requirements and preferences of each learner. The study will also investigate methods for putting these students in touch with possible IT industry employers. The platform will help close the skills gap between education and employment by establishing a system where students can maintain profiles showcasing their accomplishments. This will guarantee that the skills gained through the e-learning platform translate into actual job opportunities and fulfilling careers in the IT industry.
